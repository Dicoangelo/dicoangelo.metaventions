## Core Philosophy

### "Be Impeccable With Your Word"

Drawn from ancient wisdom and personal reflection, this principle guides everything:

- **Words create reality**: Every commitment, every promise, every technical specification is treated as sacred. When I say something will ship, it ships.
- **Precision over volume**: Fewer words, more meaning. Code comments cite papers. Documentation is infrastructure.
- **No black magic**: I don't use words to manipulate or deceive. Technical communication is clear, honest, and actionable.

**How this manifests in work:**
- Commit messages that explain *why*, not just *what*
- Documentation that respects the reader's time
- Promises kept, or renegotiated transparently before deadline

### Systems Over Goals

Goals are outcomes. Systems are the daily actions that compound into outcomes.

**Philosophy:**
- "I don't rise to the level of my goals. I fall to the level of my systems."
- Build habits, processes, and infrastructure that make success inevitable
- Measure leading indicators, not just lagging results

**Applied:**
- 700+ error patterns in recovery engine → systematic debugging
- 114 research sessions archived → systematic learning
- DQ scoring for routing decisions → systematic quality control

## Values & What Drives Me

### Meaningful Impact Over Optimization

I'm not interested in making ads 0.1% more clickable. I want to work on problems that matter:

- **AI that augments human capability**, not replaces it
- **Systems that scale knowledge**, not just transactions
- **Infrastructure that compounds**, not depletes
- **Technology that serves humanity**, not exploits it

### Ownership Over Execution

The difference between a contractor and an owner:
- Contractor: "I did what was asked"
- Owner: "I ensured the outcome was achieved"

I want to own outcomes, not just tasks. This means:
- Understanding the *why* behind requirements
- Pushing back when something won't work
- Going beyond scope when it serves the mission
- Taking responsibility when things go wrong

### Future Over Legacy

I'm energized by what's emerging, not what's established:
- New paradigms (multi-agent systems, not monolithic models)
- Emerging technologies (voice AI, biometric interfaces)
- Unsolved problems (enterprise knowledge architecture)
- Systems that don't exist yet

This doesn't mean ignoring lessons from the past—it means applying them to build the future.

### Depth Over Breadth

I'd rather know one domain deeply than many superficially:
- 8+ papers implemented in multi-agent systems (not 80 papers skimmed)
- 127K LOC in one platform (not 12 half-finished projects)
- 4 years at one company (not 4 jobs in 4 years)

Depth compounds. Breadth dissipates.
