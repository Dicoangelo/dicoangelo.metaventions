### "How do you prioritize when everything is urgent?"

**I use a framework: Impact × Urgency × Dependency, then communicate trade-offs transparently.**

**The framework:**

1. **Impact:** What's the business/user value if this ships?
   - High: Blocks revenue, affects users, enables other work
   - Medium: Improves experience, reduces costs
   - Low: Nice-to-have, cosmetic

2. **Urgency:** What's the real deadline?
   - Critical: Production down, customer commitment, regulatory
   - High: Scheduled launch, dependent work blocked
   - Medium: Internal goal, optimization
   - Low: Backlog item, future consideration

3. **Dependency:** What's blocked by this?
   - Blocking: Other people/systems can't proceed
   - Enabling: Unlocks parallel work
   - Independent: Self-contained

**Example from Contentsquare:**

**Situation:** Q4 2024—3 concurrent urgent requests:
1. AWS Quarterly Partner Review (QPR) deck due in 2 days
2. Microsoft co-sell pipeline data needed for executive meeting tomorrow
3. PartnerStack sync failing (300 partners can't register deals)

**Prioritization:**
1. **Fix PartnerStack sync** (Impact: High, Urgency: Critical, Dependency: Blocking 300 partners)
   - 2 hours to diagnose and patch
2. **Microsoft pipeline data** (Impact: High, Urgency: Critical, Dependency: Executive decision)
   - 1 hour to pull data, 30 min to format
3. **AWS QPR deck** (Impact: Medium, Urgency: High, Dependency: Independent)
   - Negotiated 1-day extension, delivered with higher quality

**Result:** All three delivered, no missed commitments, stakeholders informed throughout.
